M13 Hercules Globular Cluster | 2012/june/1st

Shot with a Celestron Ultima 8 with focal reducer, Canon EOS 1100D and LPR filter. 20 frames, 32 seconds each, at 3200 ISO.

No darks, no flats, no bias.

North is up.

Note: I changed guiding method from PhD to MetaGuide.

I suffered from apparently "out of focus" stars, blaming either my C8, the focal reducer or the focusing procedure (I don't use masks, yet).

After shooting unguided very short exposures of M13 (for framing) I realise that the images were (far) better than the guided ones.

Looking for an alternative I came across MetaGuide that works similarly to PhD (à la "dummy, press here")

It might be just that I use default settings for guiding, and could be that the default settings are better suited to MetaGuide than for PhD.

I have yet to work on better than default values on that matter, since it might do a difference.
